Keratinocyte state-level F2RL1 analysis

Subclustering resolved 11 keratinocyte states from the paired keratinocyte manuscript cohort.
Marker-based labels indicate a mixture of differentiated, basal, junctional, stress-response, cycling, mesenchymal-like, and likely contaminant states.
Among epithelial keratinocyte states, the most LE-enriched state was KC3 Differentiated KC (mean abundance delta 4.11 pp).
The most LE-depleted epithelial state was KC0 Basal KC (mean abundance delta -7.93 pp).
The epithelial state with the largest increase in within-state F2RL1-positive fraction in LE was KC10 Mesenchymal-like KC (3.90 pp).
The epithelial state with the largest decrease in within-state F2RL1-positive fraction in LE was KC8 KRT7/KRT18-like KC (-12.78 pp).

State-signal decomposition of the overall LE - Normal shift:
- Overall F2RL1-positive fraction delta: -5.24 pp, composed of -0.62 pp from state abundance changes and -4.62 pp from within-state F2RL1 shifts.
- Overall mean-expression delta: -0.1296, composed of -0.0021 from state abundance changes and -0.1274 from within-state expression shifts.
- Largest epithelial contributor to the percent-positive difference: KC2 Stress-response KC (-7.17 pp total; composition -3.73 pp, within-state -3.44 pp).
- Largest epithelial contributor to the mean-expression difference: KC2 Stress-response KC (-0.3199 total; composition -0.1865, within-state -0.1335).

Contaminant sensitivity of the overall F2RL1-positive fraction:
- All keratinocytes: Normal 41.62% vs LE 36.39% (paired t p=0.0759)
- Excluding immune-like contaminants: Normal 42.64% vs LE 36.11% (paired t p=0.0803)
- Epithelial core only: Normal 43.48% vs LE 37.08% (paired t p=0.0795)

Interpretation:
The reverse scRNA trend is not explained by obvious contaminant clusters alone.
Instead, F2RL1 appears heterogeneous across keratinocyte states, so tissue-level bulk RNA or protein assays may diverge from a simple single-cell percent-positive metric if state composition changes between Normal and LE.
